Key Insights

1. The ace gap is real but overstated. Sinner averages 5.9 aces per match to Alcaraz's 4.29, but the bigger differentiator is the 4% gap in first-serve points won. Sinner's flatter serve generates weaker returns, setting up shorter rallies that suit his baseline game.
2. Alcaraz's approach is accuracy-first. Hitting 65% of first serves in means fewer second serves exposed to attack. Alcaraz's 2.3 double faults per match vs Sinner's 1.89 shows the tradeoff — Sinner goes bigger but misses more.
3. Sinner is the better escape artist. At 68% break points saved vs 64%, Sinner is more reliable under pressure on serve — a critical edge in tight matches where every service game matters.
